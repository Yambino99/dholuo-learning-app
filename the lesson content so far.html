üìö Theory: Personal Pronouns

In Dholuo, there are two distinct types of personal pronouns: Isolated pronouns and inseparable pronouns.

Isolated pronouns can stand alone, while inseparable pronouns must always be attached to a verb.

Isolated Pronouns

An
English: I/Me
In
English: You (singular)
En
English: He/Him or She/Her

Grammar Note: Pronouns in dholuo aren't gendered.

Wan
English: We
Un
English: You (plural)
Gin
English: They/Them

üìö Theory: Basic Greetings

In Dholuo culture, greetings are very important and show respect. Let's learn the most common greetings:

Misawa
Literal: "Good morning"
English: Good morning
Oyawore
Literal: "Good evening"
English: Good evening
Amosi
Literal: "I greet you"
English: Hello/I greet you

Grammar Note: Dholuo greetings often include the time of day. The response to "Amosi" is typically "Amosi bende" (I greet you too).
